🎹 How to use Chromatrack

A generative step sequencer — open in any browser, no install needed.

Getting Started
  • Hit Play to start. Click any cell to activate a step.
  • Left-click a cell to toggle it on/off at 100% probability.
  • Right-click an empty cell to set a probable step (plays sometimes).
  • Right-click an active cell to open Step Lock (pitch, decay, wave per step).
  • Click + drag across cells to paint or erase quickly.
Row Controls (left side of grid)
  • Color dot — click to pick a custom row color
  • ~ ^ W Π * FM Pu De Ch — waveform
  • P S M L ∞ — decay length
  • «2 «1 ·· »1 »2 — octave shift
  • Number (4–16) — polymetric row length
  • Euclidean (–/1–8) — auto-distribute beats evenly
  • M / channel# — MIDI channel override
  • · / M / S — mute / solo
  • ½ · 2 4 — speed multiplier per row
  • ↑ ↓ ↕ ? — arpeggiator mode
  • Chord type — voicing applied to every note in that row
  • 🔓/🔒 — lock row from Mutate/Random
  • R· / D· — reverb / delay send
  • Filter cutoff + resonance — per-row lowpass filter
Patterns & Generative Tools
  • Patterns 1–8 — switch patterns; switches happen at the next bar while playing.
  • Copy — click Copy then a destination pattern to duplicate.
  • Chain — auto-advance through patterns 1→2→3…
  • Song — arrange patterns with custom repeat counts.
  • Mutate — randomly evolve the current pattern.
  • Random — generate a sparse random pattern from scratch.
  • Transpose ▲▼ — shift pitch up/down in semitones.
